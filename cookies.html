Cookie Policy

Paul Robinson (“we,” “our”) uses cookies and tracking technologies on our website and digital services. We only use cookies and tracking technologies with the consent of our users, except where they are strictly necessary for the operation of our services. This policy applies to Paul Robinson’s website (www.paulrobinson.com), mobile applications, and other digital properties that reference or link to this policy (collectively, “Digital Services”).

For more information about our privacy practices, please see our Privacy Policy.

1. Cookies and Online Tracking Technologies

Web Browser Cookies

A web browser cookie is a small text file stored on your device by a website. Cookies may store information like your IP address, browser type, and preferences, helping websites remember settings and analyze how users interact with services.

Other Tracking Technologies

  • Web Beacons/GIFs, Pixels, and Page Tags: Small transparent images or code in emails or apps to track interactions and improve services.
  • Mobile App Tracking: Mobile apps may collect unique device identifiers, activity logs, and location data to enhance functionality and personalise experiences.

2. How We Use Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and tracking technologies for:

  • Strictly Necessary Purposes: To enable essential features, such as remembering login information, user preferences, and portal functionality.
  • Performance Monitoring: To generate statistics about how users interact with our Digital Services. This includes Google Analytics 4, which collects anonymised data, such as approximate location, language, and user interactions, while ensuring IP anonymisation.
  • Functional Features: To tailor content based on your location or preferences.
  • Advertising: With your consent, we may use tools like LinkedIn Insight to measure and improve the effectiveness of our campaigns.

For details on Google Analytics 4 and its data collection practices, visit Google Analytics Information.

3. Your Choices: Managing Cookies

Your Preferences

When you visit our Digital Services, a cookie banner allows you to accept or reject cookies (except those strictly necessary for operation). You can change your preferences at any time via the “Cookie Settings” button.

Managing Browser and Device Settings

You can control how cookies are handled via your browser or device settings. Note that disabling cookies may limit functionality on our Digital Services.

Learn how to manage cookies for popular browsers:

  • Cookie settings in Chrome
  • Cookie settings in Firefox
  • Cookie settings in Internet Explorer
  • Cookie settings in Safari
